## What is The Mighty Small Team?

AI is reshaping team structure. Teams that used to need 10-15 people (frontend, backend, QA, ops, PM, design...) can now be covered by 3-5 people working with AI Agents. This isn't just "downsizing" — it's role compression and reorganization:

- **Everyone is a generalist** — with AI, one person can handle development, testing, and deployment
- **Dynamic roles** — no more fixed "frontend engineer" or "backend engineer"; roles shift based on the task
- **Full autonomy** — small teams own the entire pipeline from requirements to production

The community has engaged in ongoing discussion about "how teams should be structured in the AI era": traditional functional divisions are breaking down, replaced by small, delivery-oriented, autonomous units.

## Why It Matters

Large teams mean high communication overhead — each additional person causes communication paths to grow exponentially. Once AI multiplies individual capability, small teams can actually deliver faster and more flexibly than large ones. This isn't just an engineering team change — it's the direction of organizational evolution.
