<h2 style="font-size:18px;font-weight:700;letter-spacing:-0.01em;margin:0 0 14px;padding-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid var(--color-surface-border)">My Story</h2>
<div style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.9;color:var(--color-text-secondary);margin-bottom:28px">
  <p style="margin:0 0 14px">Born in 1992, civil engineering major, worked in the construction industry after graduation. <strong style="color:var(--color-text-primary)">Bridge cracks mean lives at stake</strong> — that reverence for consequences carried into software. Most software engineers reference only the software industry when discussing "engineering management." Civil engineering's standardization, supervision systems, change management, and cost control offer a completely different perspective.</p>
  <p style="margin:0 0 14px">Switched to coding in 2014 — Android, iOS, backend, web, all of it. Led my first project in 2017 and experienced <strong style="color:var(--color-text-primary)">project collapse and team chaos</strong>. Took over the entire R&D department in 2019, growing from 0 to 60+ people, and became VP of Engineering in 2025. Every pitfall I fell into eventually became judgment.</p>
  <p style="margin:0 0 14px">Nearly eight years in management, still coding at high frequency. Built microservice architectures, delivered YOLO image recognition end-to-end. Technical judgment comes from writing code, not from hearing about it.</p>
</div>
